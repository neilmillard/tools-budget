# The 7th Cure: Increase Thy Ability to Earn

In the final cure of Babylon, Arkad teaches the **7th Cure: "Increase thy ability to earn."** He tells his students that the most valuable asset they have is their own knowledge and skills.

For a long time, I focused entirely on the first six cures. I was obsessed with saving 10%, cutting my expenses, and investing my gold. But I realized I was missing the most powerful lever of all. 

## The Ceiling of Frugality
There is a limit to how much you can cut your expenses. You can't live on zero. But there is no theoretical limit to how much you can increase your ability to earn. 

I remember the moment I decided to learn how to build software. It was my "Wait, really?" moment for the 7th Cure. By investing a few months of my evenings into a new skill, I didn't just "save" money—I fundamentally changed the math of my entire life. I created these tools, and in doing so, I increased my value to the world. 

## Modern Ways to Increase Your Earning Power
Arkad's advice to the Babylonian artisan is just as relevant today:

1. **Master Your Tools:** Don't just "use" the software at your job. Master it. Automate the boring parts. Be the person who knows how to leverage technology to get ten times more done.
2. **Continuous Learning:** I try to spend at least one hour a day learning something new. It's the only investment that is guaranteed to pay dividends.
3. **Build Your Network:** Arkad suggests "exchanging knowledge with others." Some of my biggest career leaps came from simple conversations with people more skillful than me.

## Fueling the Operating System
I built this site to provide **"Ancient wisdom. Modern tools."** but the tools are just multipliers. They need fuel. 

Your earning capacity is the fuel for your financial operating system. The more you earn (while keeping your expenditures controlled), the faster you can move through the 4-stage journey. 

## Your Path Forward
If you feel stuck in your financial journey, stop looking at your savings for a moment and look at yourself. What skill could you learn this year that would make you more valuable? 

Investing in yourself is the only investment that can never be stolen, taxed, or lost to inflation.
