# Inflation: The Invisible Thief in Your Purse

The fourth cure shared by Arkad in ancient Babylon is: **"Guard thy treasures from loss."** In his day, this meant avoiding bad loans, shady business partners, and outright scams. Today, we face a much more subtle and persistent threat: **Inflation**.

For a long time, I thought "safe" meant keeping my money in a high-street bank account. I liked seeing the number stay the same. I thought I was being prudent. I thought I was "guarding my treasures." 

I was wrong. I was watching my wealth evaporate.

## The "Safe" Loss: A Mathematical Illusion
I realized that if my money was sitting in an account earning 1% interest while the price of bread, milk, and rent was rising by 4%, I wasn't "guarding" anything. I was losing 3% of my purchasing power every single year. 

Inflation is like a tiny, invisible leak in your purse. If you have £1,000 today, and inflation is 3%, that same £1,000 will only buy you £970 worth of goods next year. In ten years, it buys you about £740. In twenty years, it’s worth nearly half. 

If you don't account for this, your "safe" savings are actually a guaranteed loss. This is a direct violation of the 4th Cure.

## My "Wait, Really?" Moment with Real Returns
The turning point for me was running the numbers on my own retirement plan. I had a target number in my head—let’s say £500,000—that I thought was "enough" to live a comfortable life. 

Then I adjusted that number for 25 years of 3% inflation. **The result was staggering.** To have the same lifestyle that £500,000 buys today, I would actually need over £1,000,000 in 25 years. 

My "enough" was actually only worth about half of what I thought it would be. That was the moment I realized that cash is not a store of value over long periods; it's a medium of exchange that is designed to lose value. To truly guard my treasures, I had to move them from cash into productive assets that could multiply (the 3rd Cure).

## The Psychology of Nominal vs. Real
Humans are prone to something called **Money Illusion**. We see the number on our bank statement go up (nominal value) and we feel richer. But the only number that matters is what that money can *buy* (real value). 

If your salary goes up by 5% but inflation is 7%, you have actually taken a 2% pay cut. You are working just as hard (or harder) for less stuff. Once you start viewing the world through the lens of "Real Returns," your entire investment strategy changes.

## How I Guard My Treasures Now
Guarding your treasures today requires a multi-layered shield:

1. **Cash is for the Short Term:** I keep my emergency fund (Stage 2) in cash. I accept that this money is losing value to inflation, but I pay that "tax" for the benefit of liquidity. It's the price of insurance.
2. **Productive Assets are the Shield:** History shows that only productive assets—things that produce something or have intrinsic utility—consistently beat inflation over decades. This means **Stage 4: Investing** in global equities and real estate. These assets can raise their prices or rents as inflation rises, protecting your purchasing power.
3. **The Pension Engine:** Your pension is your biggest defense against the invisible thief. Because it’s invested in the global economy and benefits from massive tax relief, it has the best chance of growing faster than the cost of living.
4. **Avoid 'Gilt' Edged Scams:** In the search for safety, many people turn to low-yielding bonds or "guaranteed" products that barely match inflation. Don't let the word "guaranteed" blind you to the fact that you are guaranteed to lose purchasing power.
